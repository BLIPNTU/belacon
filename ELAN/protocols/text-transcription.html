General transcription protocol & transcription resources

A General Step by Step Guide to Text Transcription for Corpus Data (Standard Version)

  1. Launch ELAN

  2. Import the media file(s) and the template file (if available). Refer to the Transcription Template section of the wiki for instructions on how to handle the template subsequent to importing it.

  3. Go to “File” and click “Save” or “Save As” like a normal document. The file should be named according to the file naming conventions given to you. You can also set an automatic backup of the file by going to “File” > “Automatic Backup”. Choose a time interval between 1 to 30 minutes that suits your preference. Note: the backup file will be saved with the extension *.eaf.001.

  4. Listen to the media file in full before you begin transcribing. Familiarize yourself with the participants’ voices. Note how many participants there are. Note what languages are being spoken. Note any muffled parts or parts where all speech becomes indecipherable. Information on how to deal with these parts can be found under Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ). Note the start and end of any activities (if applicable) for the purposes of the activity marker tier.

  5. We will identify each tier by the participant. Identify the participant by their relationship to the baby in brackets. For example, the mother is just “Mother” and the baby is just “Baby”.

  6. Rename the tier attributes to replace placeholder participant codes with accurate participant ID codes for all participants present in the recording. Go to Tier > Change Tier Attributes > Edit the “Participant” line. Ensure you also edit the Researcher’s participant line and tier name to reflect their actual researcher code (if they are present in the recording).

  7. Generate any new participant tiers if necessary (e.g., if there’s an Unknown individual or a sibling). See Transcribing Dependent Tiers in the wiki for instructions on how to create new dependent tiers. See A Guide to all your tiers in the wiki for the tier type stereotypes and hierarchical relationships needed for each tier. Consult your supervisor if you are having issues.

  8. Adjust the vertical zoom (right click on the waveform > Vertical Zoom > adjust the %) and horizontal zoom (adjust the slider at the bottom right of the ELAN screen) to help you see the waveform clearly.

  9. Put on your headphones and begin transcribing! Refer to the wiki for what you need to know regarding our transcription conventions.
